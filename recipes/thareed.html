What is Thareed

Thareed (or Tharid) is a Middle Eastern dish consisting of red meat chunks with vegetables and thick sauce from tomatoes and tomato paste, cooked slowly, and served over pita bread or flatbread. The term literally means “stewed beef with vegetables over bread.”

Ingredients

  • Red meat
  • vegetables
  • Spices
  • Arabic bread

Cooking Tips

  1. Use ghee instead of any cooking oil. If you are overwhelmed with the richness of ghee, you can mix 1 tbsp ghee with 2 tbsp of cooking oil and you are good to go.
  2. Any Arabic spice blend is good for this dish. You can even create your own. Sometimes, I feel that the secret of the entire dish is from the Arabic spices.
  3. Fry onion, rather than just sautee, i.e., lightly brown the onion during the initial steps of cooking for a more defined taste. The use of garlic is optional (my mother-in-law does not use it) as it adds aroma to the dish as well.
